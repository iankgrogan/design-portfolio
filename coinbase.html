Connecting Crypto

Creating a community feature for one of the world's most popular crypto exchanges, Coinbase.

Type
Portfolio / Concept Project
Role
UX / Product Designer
Tools
Figma, Coinbase Design System (recreated)
Focus
Mobile-first · Design Systems · Community UX
Overview

Stepping outside the SaaS box

This is a portfolio-building project designed to step out of my usual world of SaaS logistics applications and design a new feature for an existing mobile application. As a Coinbase user myself, I've long wanted a feature where I could connect with other users to better understand market sentiment around specific assets.

Goal

Community-driven sentiment

If this were a real project, the goal would be to provide a community where Coinbase users can connect with each other around an asset of their interest — resulting in increased time spent viewing a particular asset and a higher likelihood of executing more transactions on the platform.

Connecting users around shared interests increases engagement and ultimately benefits both the user (better information) and the business (more transactions).

Personas

Retail crypto investors

Personas for this project are loosely based around myself and friends who are retail crypto investors using Coinbase as their main exchange. Even for this concept project, defining personas kept focus on priorities for the design.

Key factors captured: crypto investment style, frequency of checking a particular asset on Coinbase, and where/how they consume crypto news and information.

Wireframes

Proof of concept in lo-fi

Using Figma, I created low-fidelity wireframes. These were used for gathering initial feedback as a proof of concept, as well as planning the general direction for the solution.

Low-fidelity wireframes used for initial feedback

User Feedback

Users were overall receptive to the first iteration and felt the concept was viable. One piece of criticism raised was around the sentiment buttons in the post drawer — which I addressed in subsequent iterations.

Design System

Designing within an existing system

This project was a strong test of my ability to work within an already-defined design system. I recreated a mini design kit from the components needed to complete the UIs — matching Coinbase's visual language closely enough that users felt it fit seamlessly into the existing experience.

Recreated mini design kit — components matching Coinbase's design language

UI Design

High-fidelity and user-validated

These high-fidelity mockups were shown to users for final feedback. The response was consistently that the design felt like a natural extension of the Coinbase app.

High-fidelity mockups — validated by users as fitting seamlessly within Coinbase

Some users suggested adding gif and image sharing as well. If this were a real project, those would be strong candidates for post-MVP features.

Takeaways

Designing mobile-first, with constraint

This project was a great exercise in the mobile-first mindset. I've designed for mobile before, but never as the primary focus — and the constraints of a small screen sharpened every design decision.

It's also a strong representation of my ability to work within an existing design system and create something that fits in seamlessly — exactly what users confirmed. I'm happy with the overall solution and would have loved to put it in front of a larger group for more feedback.

The clearest skill I sharpened here: knowing when to follow the design system's rules and when to use good judgment to serve the user's needs — without breaking the overall experience.
